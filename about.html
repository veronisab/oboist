Veronica Isabelle Ulrikkeholm (born Stubberud) is a Norwegian oboist, based in Drammen/Oslo, Norway, having just relocated from Copenhagen, Denmark. She is enjoying a rich freelance career, regularly performing with various orchestras and ensembles in Norway, Denmark and abroad.

In 2019, and until the early days of the pandemic, Veronica lived in London, where she was a scholar at the Royal College of Music, and did a Post-graduate Artist Diploma supported by the Mimi Opperby Award. She studied with the two principal oboists of the London Symphony Orchestra, Juliana Koch and Olivier Stankiewicz, as well as principal oboist of Teatro alla Scala Orchestra, Fabien Thouand, Christine Pendrill (principal cor anglais, London Symphony Orchestra) and Jacques Tys from the Paris Conservatoire. She has also studied with Johannes Grosso and Min Hua Chiu in Oslo.

Veronica holds a Master of Music degree from the Royal Danish Academy of Music in Copenhagen, where she studied with Max Artved and Joakim Dam Thomsen; and a bachelor degree from the Royal Academy of Music Aarhus, where she studied under principal oboist of the Aarhus Symphony Orchestra, Oliver Nordahl.
